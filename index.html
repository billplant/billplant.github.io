Four Steps to your New Career

From start to finish, here are the steps you'll be taking towards qualifying as a Driving Instructor

Registration

To teach within the UK you need to pass a background check and register with the government.

Part 1: Theory & Hazard Perception

A 1hr 45 minute test of your knowledge of The Highway Code, road theory, the law and perception of hazards

Part 2: Driving

A 1 hr test which includes an eyesight test, vehicle safety questions and a test of your driving ability

Part 3: Teaching

A 1 hr test, with a real pupil, of your instructional ability

PART 1:
Theory & Hazard Perception

BOOK PART 1

Theory - Multiple Choice questions

You'll be asked 100 questions in total. 25 from each of these 4 categories:
- Road Procedures
- Traffic signs/signals, car control and mechanical knowledge
- Driving test, disabilities and the law
- Publications and instructional techniques

To pass you need to score at least 20 out of 25 in each category and at least 85 out of 100 in total.

Hazard Perception - CGI Video clips

You'll be shown 14 clips that feature everyday road scenes. There will be:
- 1 Developing hazard in 13 of the clips
- 2 Developing hazards in 1 of the clips
You will be scored up to 5 for each hazard. The earlier you notive the developing hazard, the higher you score.

To pass you need to score at least 57 out of 75.

PART 2

Your Driving Ability

You already know how to drive! Part 2 training is all about bringing your driving back up to the standard needed to pass a driving test. Your instructor will help you identify any bad habits you might have allowed to creep in over the years.

The test also includes an eyesight test and a series of 'Show Me, Tell Me' Questions.

You can take the test in your own car (if it meets DVSA rules) or you can hire an instructors car for £75.

Your Instructional Ability

Now the hard work begins!
Your training (42 hours) will be focused on your ability to teach.

Throughout your training your instructor will guide you through a variety of techniques to cover Core Competencies, Lesson Planning, Risk Management and Teaching & Learning Strategies.

Our Part 3 Lesson Plans and Diagrams Book (recently updated to reflect the changes to the Part 3 test) is an invaluable tool to support your training. This can be downloaded for free or, if preferred, you can buy a fully laminated and bound hard copy for £25 (incl P&P). Just give us a call to order.

You may also decide, after you finish your Part 3 training, to gain practical experience with a trainee licence. We can help with that. Please call Matt on 01765 645023 to discuss.

How the Part 3 Test is marked

Marks between 0 and 3 are awarded in 17 categories to give you a total score. You must score at least an 8 in the 'Risk Management' section.


The lesson themes are:
- Junctions    - Rural Roads    - Town & City Driving    - Motorways    - Interacting with other road users    - Eco-safe driving
- Dual carriageway/faster moving roads    - Recap a manouevre    - Defensive Driving    - Commentary Driving
- Effective use of mirrors    - Recap emergency stop    - Independent driving    - Other

Trainee Licence

Gain practical experience (and start earning) before qualifying

Now available - Trainee (PDI) franchise! With the Part 3 recently changing to become a much more practical exam (an observed 1 hour lesson conducted by you with a real learner/driver), we are now offering a trainee franchise. This gives you the perfect opportunity to build your confidence with real learners before taking your Part 3 test.

And you could be earning up to £26.95ph as well!

The DVSA allow you to teach as a trainee instructor for 6 months (extensions are possible too). You would be expected to complete an additional 20 hours of training within the first 3 months of your licence. We have a range of options to help with this.

We'll offer you a franchise, at a very competitive rate, to ensure you have a premium vehicle (BMW 1 Series) and a supply of pupils. You must complete at least 40 hours of Part 3 training first, and be signed off by your instructor.

As you progress with your training we would encourage you to discuss this option with your instructor. See below for the forms to use, or please contact Matt for more information.

ADI21T Form

To be completed with your instructor, to confirm that you have completed at least 40 hours of Part 3 training

ADI3L Form

To be part-completed by yourself and then sent to us with the 21T, to allow us to show the DVSA that we'll sponsor you

DVSA Online Application

Once your sponsor form is signed, you can complete the licence application by making payment and confirming your badge photos
